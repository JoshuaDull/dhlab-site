The grants are structured around three-week engagements between Digital Humanities Lab staff and the recipient(s), with each week driven by a different phase of project planning: requirements drafting, design, and development.

Requirements Document This stage is all about articulating and confirming the feature that will be implemented. During this stage, the recipient commits to meeting with Lab staff once at the beginning of the week to review the Lean Canvas and Ideation forms that accompanied the application and once at the end of the week to sign off on a wireframe that will depict the requirements of the project. The recipient's signature is required before work will advance.

Design During the design phase, the Lab team will iterate different views of the application based on the signed wireframe. The recipient commits to meeting once during the middle of the week to review mockups and select one to be implemented.

Development In this final phase, the Lab team will create the feature based on the design from week 2. The recipient commits to meeting once during the middle of the week for an update on the work and to consult on any questions that have arisen.

By the end of the grant, recipients will have both the agreed-upon project feature and also experience with the production workflow that underlies many digital humanities projects

Applications must be submitted via the Rapid Prototyping Grants Google form. This form will ask for the information below.

  • Name of applicant(s)
  • Department/unit and affiliation
  • Project proposal, not to exceed 250 words. The proposal should provide:
    • An overview of the larger project and its aims
    • A detailed description of the feature that would be implemented during the grant period
    • A statement of your familiarity with the technical requirements of the project
  • A completed Lean Canvas
  • A completed Ideation form
  • Screenshots of two examples of projects that contain something similar to what you're hoping to see.
