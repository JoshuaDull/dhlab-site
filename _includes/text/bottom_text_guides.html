If you're new to digital humanities and are interested in starting a project, we welcome you to visit us in Sterling 315 during office hours.

We also highly recommend checking out other digital humanities projects so you can look for new ideas for your own research. In particular, we recommend checking out projects by the following digital humanities centers:

  • Stanford's Literary Lab
  • Northeastern's NULab for Maps, Texts, and Networks
  • Maryland's Institute for Tecnology in the Humanities
  • DHCommons Projects

{% endcapture %} {% capture right_text %}

The DHLab offers a number of awards to support digital humanities research, and we regularly consult on digital humanities research projects during weekly office hours.

In addition to on-campus resources, there are a wide range of off-campus resources that can help support digital humanities research. The following research summits all offer opportunities for researchers to continue developing their technical skills and digital projects:

  • Digital Humanities Summer Institute
  • Humanities Intensive Learning and Teaching
  • DH@Oxford
  • European Summer School in the Humanities
