Combining exploratory cultural research with the massive scale of computer-driven research, Digital Humanities research at Yale offers a safe space to exeriment with new different techniques to pursue cultural research questions.

As both a technical and theoretical enterprise, Digital Humanities touches on a wide range of extant scholarly techniques and disciplines. From Cyborg Studies to Computer Science, the range of scholarly disciplines that inform Digital Humanities research continues to grow.

Leveraging a wide range of techniques from neural networks to 3d immersive realities, Digital Humanities projects at Yale bring innovative technologies to bear on long-standing historical and cultural questions.

{% endcapture %} {% capture right_text_one %}

When beginning a Digital Humanities project, it's important to start by clearly defining your research question, and then deciding how technology can best help you answer that question. Rather than push your data collection through one or more extant tools, it's helpful to clearly articulate your research question and then decide what kind of algorithmic approach (if any) can help you pursue that research question.

If you need help clearly outlining your research questions or deciding whether algorithmic analysis can help you answer those research questions, feel free to stop by the Digital Humanities Lab during office hours on Tuesdays and Wednesdays from 2-4. We'll be happy to discuss your project and some of the methods and tools that might be appropriate for pursuing that project.
